Organization Profile

THINK AND ACT FOR TANZANIA (TATA) is a Non-Governmental Organization registered under the Ministry of Health, Community Development, Gender, Elders, Women's and Children with registration number OONGO/R/0371.

Registration

OONGO/R/0371
August 06, 2019

Area of Operation

Tanzania Mainland
Focus: People with Disabilities

Thematic Areas

Education, Health, Agriculture, Good Governance, Road Safety

Motto

"Think Big, Start Small, Be Part of Change"

Mission: Work with Tanzanian citizens in the aspects of health, education, agriculture, good governance, and road safety in order to spearhead rural development for improving quality of life.

Vision: To have a community whose do prosper socially, economically, and hygienically and that are able to reduce ignorance, diseases, and road accidents, hence enjoy a fulfilled and prolonged life.

Organization Constitution

THINK AND ACT FOR TANZANIA (TATA) is governed by a comprehensive constitution that outlines the organization's structure, operations, and guiding principles.

Article 1: Name Article 3: Preamble Article 4: Registration Article 5: Head Office Article 6: Area of Operation Article 10: Membership Article 14-17: Office Bearers Article 18-19: Board of Directors Article 20-23: General Meetings Article 25-27: Funds & Accounts Article 28-30: Amendments & Dissolution

Article 1: Name

The name of the Organization shall be THINK AND ACT FOR TANZANIA (TATA)

Article 3: Preamble

TATA as a local development initiative believes that the role to bring about change to the people and nation is ours. The motto "Think Big, Start Small, Be Part of Change" guides our work.

Article 5: Head Office

The head office is based at Mbeza Street, Foroforo Kiuzai Ward, Korogwe District, Tanga Region, P.O. Box 584.

Article 9: Objectives

1. Promote community awareness on good governance
2. Raising awareness to community on education and health
3. Facilitate Tanzania citizens to support Albinos, deaf, dumb, cripples, PLWHIV, children, and women
4. Advocate and support community in provision of social services

Article 10-12: Membership

Membership is voluntary and open to any individual of 18 years and above. Types: Founder Members and Ordinary Members.

Article 14-16: Office Bearers

Chairperson: Presides over meetings, spokesman, signatory
Secretary General: Calls meetings, keeps documents, signatory
Treasurer: Keeps money, prepares budgets, signatory

Article 18-19: Board of Directors

Composed of Chairperson, Executive Secretary, Treasurer, and appointed members. Holds meetings twice a year, quorum 2/3.

Article 20-23: General Meetings

Supreme organ composed of all members. Annual General Meeting held once a year. Quorum: 2/3 of members.

Article 25-27: Funds & Accounts

Sources: Entry fees, fundraising, grants, donations, income generating activities. Bank account with three signatories.

Article 29: Dissolution

May be dissolved by 2/3 majority vote. Assets transferred to another NGO with similar objectives.

Quarterly Reports

January - March 2020 Report

Report submitted to: Korogwe District Executive Director
Date: April 06, 2020

Activities Implemented

  • Identifying people with special needs
  • Conducting discussions with people with disabilities
  • Understanding their lives and challenges
  • Community awareness on rights

Areas Covered

  • Bungu Ward: Mheza, Kwakibomi
  • Magoma Ward: Kwemazandu, Kerenge Kibaoni
  • Mombo Ward: Mazinde Mheza, Bagamoyo, Chekelei, Makuyuni

Key Findings

Educational Challenges

  • Many didn't complete primary/secondary education
  • Parents gave up on children's education
  • Low awareness about inclusive education

Health Challenges

  • Lack of assistive devices
  • Limited access to government health services
  • Need for prosthetics and mobility aids

Economic Challenges

  • Limited access to loans (2% from internal revenue)
  • Need for project management training
  • Inadequate statistical data on special groups

Recommendations

  • Provide training before issuing loans
  • Update the register of people with disabilities
  • Conduct in-depth research on support needs

Planned Activities (April - June 2020)

  • Continue community engagement and awareness
  • Develop proposals for donor funding
  • Expand outreach to more villages
  • Strengthen partnerships with government and other organizations

Objectives & Thematic Areas

Key Objectives

1. Good Governance

Promote community awareness on good governance, road safety, and HIV/AIDS.

2. Support Vulnerable Groups

Facilitate support for Albinos, deaf, dumb, cripples, PLWHIV, women, and children to acquire education and vocational training.

3. Awareness Raising

Raise awareness on education, health, and agriculture methodologies to fight poverty.

4. Social Services Advocacy

Advocate and support community in provision of social services.

5. Capacity Building

Train organization staff for career development and quality service delivery.

6. Research

Conduct research and apply findings to bring positive change in development.

Thematic Areas of Operation

📚 Education

Supporting access to quality education and vocational training for vulnerable groups.

🏥 Health

Promoting health awareness, hygiene practices, and access to healthcare services.

🌾 Agriculture

Empowering communities with sustainable agricultural practices and food security.

🤝 Good Governance

Building awareness on governance, human rights, and community participation.

🚦 Road Safety

Educating communities on road safety to reduce accidents and save lives.

Collaboration: TATA collaborates with Groups (IGAs), CBOs, NGOs (local and international), and other development partners in the five key areas to fight against poverty.
